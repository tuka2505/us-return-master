Holiday Return Policies 2026: Extended Deadlines Guide

Updated: February 2026 | Special Holiday Edition

The holiday season is the busiest time for shopping, and US retailers know that many gifts are bought weeks in advance. To accommodate this, most major stores offer Extended Holiday Return Windows.

1. Amazon’s Long Holiday Window

Traditionally, most items purchased on Amazon between November 1 and December 31 can be returned until January 31 of the following year. This gives gift recipients plenty of time after Christmas to decide.

2. Walmart and Target Extensions

Walmart and Target typically offer similar extensions. For items with short return windows (like 15-day electronics), the "clock" doesn't start ticking until December 26. This means an iPad bought in November is still returnable in early January.

3. Apple Store Specifics

Apple usually has a very tight 14-day policy, but during the holidays, they soften it. Items received between early November and December 25 are typically returnable through January 8.

4. What Stays the Same?

Even during the holidays, some rules don't change:

  • Final Sale items remain non-returnable.
  • Restocking fees still apply to activatable devices.
  • Proof of purchase (or a gift receipt) is still highly recommended.

Gift Tip: Always ask for a **Gift Receipt**. It allows the recipient to return the item for store credit without knowing how much you spent!
